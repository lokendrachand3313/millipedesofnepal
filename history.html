History of Nepalese Millipede Description

The history of millipede taxonomy in Nepal is rich and extensive, beginning with Carl's 1935 description of Orthomorpha (Orthomorpha) simulans and Hingstonia eremita, the latter of which established the genus Hingstonia. In 1936, Attems introduced the new genus Kophosphaera based on Kophosphaera excavata (Butler, 1874) and referenced Carl's previously mentioned species. After a long gap, Demange (1961) described two species of Gonoplectus from Nepal, namely G. hyatti and G. brolemanni, collected by K. H. Hyatt in 1954. Shear (1979) then described a new subfamily, Nepalelinnae, with the new genus Nepalella, introducing two new species, and three new species of Tianella. In 1983, Mauries described Trachyiulus wilsonae and Podoglyphiulus elegans nepalensis in the family Cambalopsidae, and later that year, he introduced a new subgenus, Nepalmatoiulus, within the genus Chromatoiulus, describing three new species and reporting Gonoplectus malayus from Nepal for the first time.

In 1986, Golovatch reported the family Polydesmidae from Nepal for the first time, describing four species of Usbekodesmus, a new genus Himalodesmus with two species, two species of Hingstonia, and four species of Solaphilus. The following year, Golovatch (1987a) reported the family Opisotretidae from Nepal, introducing the new genus Martensodesmus with three species, one species of Usbekodesmus, five species of Himalodesmus, and two species of Hingstonia. In another 1987 publication (1987b), Golovatch reported the family Glomeridae from Nepal, describing five species of the genus Hyleoglomeris and a new genus Nepalotretus, which he later synonymized with Glennia. Shear (1987) described four species of Tianella, two species of Nepalella, and one species of Kashmireuma. In the same year, Enghoff elevated Nepalmatoiulus to the genus level and described ten new species within this genus. Mauries (1988) described four species (+1) of Tianella, one species (+1) of Kashmireuma, and six species (+1) of Nepalella from Nepal.

In 1990, Golovatch recorded Orthomorpha coarctata (de Sausurre, 1860) and Oxidus gracilis (C. Koch, 1847) from Nepal for the first time, describing four species (+1) and one new record of Paranedyopus (later synonymized with Anoplodesmus), and described Orophosoma fechteri from Nepal. In 1990b, Golovatch described one species of Himalodesmus, one species (+1) of Usbekodesmus, four species (+1) of Hingstonia, and two species of Solaphilus. In 1992, Golovatch introduced a new genus, Martensosoma, with six new species and described two species of the genus Armolites. In 1993, he described one species of the genus Substrongylosoma. The following year (1994a), Golovatch described one species of Armolites and introduced two new genera, Nepalomorpha with three new species, and Parorthomorpha with eight new species. In another 1994 publication (1994b), Golovatch described a new genus, Hirtodrepanum, with new species, and a new species of the genus Touranella.

In 1996, Golovatch described 14 species under the genus Orophosoma from Nepal. That same year, Shelley described a new genus, Nepalozonium, with one species. In 2001, Korsos described three species of Anaulaciulus from Nepal, and in 2009, Korsos et al. described Hirudicryptus quintumelementum. In 2011, Golovatch synonymized the genus Usbekodesmus with Epanerchodus, transferring all species described within Usbekodesmus to Epanerchodus. In 2014, Golovatch synonymized several genera (Nepalomorpha, Parorthomorpha, Martensosoma, Armolites, Orophosoma) into Delarthrum Attems, 1936, transferring all species described from Nepal to the latter genus. He also described seven new species of Delarthrum and introduced a new genus, Beronodesmus, from Nepal with one species. Golovatch and VandenSpiegel (2014) described a new genus, Koponenius, with two new species, one of which was from Nepal.

In 2015, Golovatch reported Streptogonopus phipsoni (Pocock, 1892) from Nepal for the first time and described one species each of Anoplodesmus and Kronopolites, seven species of Beronodesmus, and four species of Delarthrum. He also introduced a new genus, Beronodesmoides, with three new species. In 2016a, Golovatch described five new species from Nepal, including two species of Substrongylosoma, one species of Pocockina, one species of Beronodesmoides, and one species of Anoplodesmus. In 2016b, he described two species, Delarthrum quadridentatum and Touranella pilosa, and one new species of Koponenius with VandenSpiegel (2016). In 2016c, Golovatch described four species of Beronodesmoides: B. typicus, B. montigena, B. martensi, and Beronodesmus simplex. Golovatch et al. (2016) described two species of Beronodesmus from Nepal. Recently, Golovatch (2022) described Epanerchodus telnovi and in 2023, he described two species, Chondromorpha greke and Delarthrum telnovi, from Nepal.

Note: (+1) = species unidentified to species level because of the absence of male specimens or geographical record.

A Brief History of Working on Millipedes

For my master's thesis, I aimed to explore a novel subject within the context of Nepal. Under the guidance of my professor and supervisor, I focused on the Diplopoda (millipedes). I conducted a comprehensive study of millipedes in urban forest patches within the Kathmandu Valley.

My research primarily involved the taxonomic description of millipedes, emphasizing the identification based on gonopod structures. The discovery of high levels of endemism in these millipedes sparked a deeper interest in the Diplopoda of Nepal, highlighting the unique biodiversity of the region.
